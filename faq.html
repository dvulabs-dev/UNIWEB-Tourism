Frequently Asked Questions

Sri Lanka is a year-round destination. The best time to visit the west and south coasts and cultural triangle is between December and April, while the east coast's best weather is from May to October.

Yes, most travelers need an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) to enter Sri Lanka. You can easily apply for this online before your travel dates, which allows for a stay of up to 30 days.

Light, breathable cotton clothing is recommended due to the tropical climate. However, if you plan to visit the hill country (like Nuwara Eliya), pack a light sweater or jacket as evenings can be chilly. Modest clothing covering shoulders and knees is required when visiting temples.

Traditional Sri Lankan food can be quite spicy, but tourist hotels and restaurants usually tone down the heat. There are also many delicious non-spicy options, such as hoppers, string hoppers, and various tropical fruits.

Yes, Sri Lanka is generally a very safe destination for tourists. However, as with any travel, we recommend taking standard precautions with your valuables and avoiding isolated areas at night.

The official currency is the Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR). Credit cards are widely accepted in hotels, restaurants, and larger shops, but it is advisable to carry some cash for small purchases, tuk-tuks, and tipping.
